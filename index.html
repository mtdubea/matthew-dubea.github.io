Welcome

This site documents my senior capstone project in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette: a lightweight, front-wheel, direct-pedal-powered racing tricycle engineered to win the 2025 Acadiana 500 relay in New Iberia, Louisiana.

The vehicle—designed, analysed, and fabricated over two semesters—took first place overall while remaining fully compliant with a strict rule set (no chains, gears, or motors).

Thanks to event coordinator Hanna Pellerin (Metal Shark Boats) and sponsors such as Raising Cane’s who supported materials and entry fees.

📏 Race Compliance – Spec Sheet

The official tricycle design constraints are summarised below (seat height, width limits, pedal placement, etc.).

📚 Race Constraints & Requirements

  • Direct pedal-to-wheel drive only (no gears / chains).
  • Human-powered only — absolutely no motors.
  • Seat height ≤ 25 in; total width ≤ 20 in.
  • Relay transitions in 30-ft zones between cones.
  • Penalty: 10 s added per violation.
