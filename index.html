Welcome

This site documents my senior capstone project in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette: a lightweight, front-wheel, direct-pedal-powered racing tricycle that won the 2025 Acadiana 500 relay in New Iberia, Louisiana.

Designed, analysed, and fabricated over two semesters, the vehicle finished first overall while complying with strict rules (no chains, gears, or motors).

Special thanks to event coordinator Hanna Pellerin (Metal Shark Boats) and sponsors such as Raising Cane’s for supporting materials and entry fees.

📚 Race Constraints & Requirements

  • Direct pedal-to-wheel drive only (no gears / chains).
  • Human-powered only — absolutely no motors.
  • Seat height ≤ 25 in; total width ≤ 20 in.
  • Relay transitions in 30 ft zones.
  • 10 s penalty per rule violation.
