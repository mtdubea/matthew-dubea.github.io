Welcome

This site presents my senior capstone project in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette: a high-performance, front-wheel, direct-pedal-powered racing tricycle, engineered to win the 2025 Acadiana 500 Tricycle Relay in New Iberia, Louisiana.

The competition required full compliance with a strict design rule set, including no chains or gears and only foot-powered front-wheel drive. Our final vehicle—built through two semesters of design, analysis, and manufacturing—achieved first place overall.

Led by UL Lafayette and event coordinator Hanna Pellerin (Metal Shark Boats), the Acadiana 500 is a sponsored event supporting engineering innovation. Special thanks to our sponsors including Raising Cane’s, who helped cover team entry fees and materials.

📏 Race Compliance – Spec Sheet

This specification sheet outlines the official tricycle design constraints required for entry into the Acadiana 500, including seat height, width limits, and pedal placement regulations.

📚 Race Constraints & Requirements

Rules were provided by the race committee to ensure safety and fairness. Key constraints included:

  • Direct pedal-to-wheel drive only (no gears, no chains)
  • Foot-powered operation only — no motors allowed
  • Max seat height: 25 inches; max trike width: 20 inches
  • Transition relays must occur in 30-ft zones between cones
  • Penalties: 10 seconds per violation added to total time
