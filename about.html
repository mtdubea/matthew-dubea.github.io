About This Project

A lightweight, front-wheel, direct-pedal-powered tricycle was designed, fabricated, and raced over a 3,400-foot asphalt course during the 2025 Acadiana 500 relay. Beginning with a salvaged 1020 steel bicycle frame, fork, and modified handlebars, our team engineered a reinforced rear axle assembly using TIG-welded 4130 tubing and a heat-treated 4140 steel shaft supported by brass bushings and sealed bearings.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) estimated a maximum von Mises stress of 75 MPa under an 800 N rider load, resulting in a factor of safety of 4.5. The static tip-over angle of 23° confirmed high lateral stability. At 35 lbs and built under a $120 budget, the trike reached test speeds of 14–15 mph. The "Fast Pedal Engineers" swept all five heats to earn 1st place overall, with no mechanical failures or penalties.
