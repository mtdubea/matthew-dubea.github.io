Project Snapshot

Our lightweight, front‑wheel direct‑drive trike tackled the 3 400‑ft asphalt Acadiana 500 relay. Starting from a salvaged 1020‑steel bike frame, we TIG‑welded a reinforced 4130 rear‑axle cradle around a heat‑treated 4140 shaft with brass bushings and sealed bearings.

SolidWorks FEA predicted 75 MPa peak von Mises stress under an 800 N rider, giving a FoS = 4.5. Static tip‑over angle was 23°, confirming excellent stability. The 35‑lb build cost under $120 and hit 14–15 mph in testing. “Fast Pedal Engineers” swept all five heats for 1ˢᵗ place—zero penalties, zero failures.
