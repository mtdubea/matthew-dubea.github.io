📝 Sample Calculations & Analysis

1. Mass, Weight & Basic Kinematics

System weight: W = m × g = 209.4 lbm × 32.174 ft/s² = 209.4 lbf (≈ 932 N for a 95 kg total mass).

Lap: L = 3485 ft, time t = 211 s → vavg = L / t = 16.5 ft/s = 11.3 mph.

Wheel circumference: C = π · 20 in = 5.24 ft → rotations / lap ≈ 665 rev.

2. Energy & Power Budget

Kinetic energy at race pace: KE = 0.5 m v² ≈ 886 ft·lbf ≈ 1.20 kJ.

Per-lap mechanical work (@ 84 W rider output): W = P t ≈ 17.7 kJ.

3. Stability & Cornering

Rear track b = 19.5 in, CG height h = 23 in → θtip = atan((b/2)/h) ≈ 23°.

Critical lateral accel: ay = g tan θ ≈ 0.42 g.

With a 15 ft radius: vmax ≈ 9.8 mph.

4. Drivetrain Cadence

Speed–cadence relationship (20 in wheel): v (mph) ≈ 0.357 × Nrpm.

  • 11.3 mph → N ≈ 32 rpm
  • 14.5 mph → N ≈ 41 rpm
5. Strength & Deflection Checks

Axle (4140 HT): τ = 903 psi, σb = 39.7 ksi → σvm ≈ 39.7 ksi, FoS ≈ 1.7.

Seat-rail deflection: δ = 0.062 in < 0.250 in spec.

Frame FEA: σvm,max = 2.795 × 10⁸ N/m², yield = 6.204 × 10⁸ N/m² → FoS = 2.22.
